# STARFORGE TCG — Phase 8: Embellishment Roadmap

**"Make It Legendary"**

All 7 launch phases are complete. StarForge has the infrastructure, the content, the economy, and the competitive systems. This phase is about the difference between a *good* game and a game people **can't stop talking about**.

---

## Guiding Principles

1. **Juice over features** — A well-animated existing feature beats a new feature with no polish.
2. **Shareability** — If a moment can't be clipped and posted, it's not dramatic enough.
3. **Collection depth** — Give collectors infinite runway. The card is the product.
4. **Social gravity** — Every feature should pull friends into the game, not just retain solo players.
5. **Respect player time** — Embellishment means delight, not grind.

---

## 8.1 Visceral Game Feel ("The Juice")

*The single biggest gap between StarForge and top-tier competitors is not features — it's how actions feel.*

### 8.1.1 Board Destruction VFX
- [ ] Heavy attacks (10+ damage) crack the board surface with animated fracture lines
- [ ] STARFORGE transformations trigger a cosmic supernova — screen flash, particle explosion, board rumbles
- [ ] Lethal blow on the enemy hero shatters the board outward in a radial explosion
- [ ] VFX intensity scales with damage dealt (3 damage = small spark, 15 damage = screen-filling blast)

### 8.1.2 Legendary Card Entrance Cinematics
- [ ] Each Legendary card gets a unique 2-3 second cinematic entrance animation
- [ ] Camera zooms to the card, golden light erupts, the card slams onto the board
- [ ] Faction-themed entrance: Pyroclast Legendaries erupt in flame, Voidborn tear a rift in space
- [ ] Audio sting unique to each Legendary (short, punchy, memorable)
- [ ] Option to skip (hold to fast-forward)

### 8.1.3 Dynamic Board Themes
- [ ] Pyroclast boards: lava flows glow brighter and fire spreads as you deal damage
- [ ] Hivemind boards: spore pods grow and pulse with each minion summoned
- [ ] Crystalline boards: crystals refract more light with each spell cast
- [ ] Voidborn boards: darkness encroaches as opponent's hand empties
- [ ] Astromancer boards: constellations form as cards are drawn
- [ ] Chronobound boards: clock mechanisms spin faster as ECHO cards are played
- [ ] Luminar boards: radiant glow intensifies with each heal
- [ ] Biotitan boards: vegetation grows and blooms as minions grow in stats
- [ ] Cogsmith boards: gears turn faster and machines assemble as Mechs are played
- [ ] Phantom Corsair boards: ghost ships sail across the background as cards are stolen

### 8.1.4 Screen Shake & Impact Frames
- [ ] 2-frame freeze on lethal blows (fighting game "hit stop")
- [ ] Screen shake intensity scales with damage (subtle for 1-2, heavy for 8+)
- [ ] Slow-motion on game-winning lethal (0.3s slow-mo → normal speed)
- [ ] Camera pull-back on board-wide AoE effects (spells that hit all minions)
- [ ] Subtle screen pulse when STARFORGE progress completes (before transformation)

### 8.1.5 Golden / Foil / Prismatic Cards
- [ ] **Golden** — Animated card art with subtle motion (flames flicker, eyes glow, particles drift)
- [ ] **Prismatic** — Holographic rainbow shimmer effect on card border + animated art
- [ ] **Astral** — Full cosmic animation: the card art is alive (creatures move, spells swirl, environments shift)
- [ ] Upgrade path: Standard → Golden → Prismatic → Astral (each tier costs progressively more Stardust)
- [ ] Animated cards render on the board during gameplay (not just in collection)
- [ ] "Diamond" indicator on card when opponent hovers, showing your premium tier

## 8.2 New Game Modes

### 8.2.1 Roguelike Dungeon Run (HIGH PRIORITY)
*The #1 most-requested mode in every TCG community. Slay the Spire proved this format is addictive.*

- [ ] Start with a weak 10-card starter deck from your chosen faction
- [ ] Face 8 increasingly difficult AI bosses with unique mechanics
- [ ] After each win, choose 1 of 3 card bundles (3 cards each) to add to your deck
- [ ] After bosses 2, 5, and 7, choose a powerful "Relic" passive (e.g., "Your GUARDIAN minions have +2 Attack", "Spells cost 1 less")
- [ ] After boss 4, remove up to 2 cards from your deck (deck thinning)
- [ ] 1 loss = run over. Rewards scale with depth reached.
- [ ] Boss roster: 24 unique bosses (8 per difficulty tier), randomly selected per run
- [ ] Leaderboard: fastest clear time, fewest cards used, most damage dealt
- [ ] Weekly "Heroic" modifier: special rules that change the run (e.g., "All minions have ECHO", "Bosses heal 5 per turn")
- [ ] Exclusive card back for clearing all 8 bosses with each faction

### 8.2.2 Lethal Puzzle Mode
- [ ] Pre-constructed board states where you must find lethal in exactly 1 turn
- [ ] 100+ handcrafted puzzles across 5 difficulty tiers (Novice → Grandmaster)
- [ ] Daily puzzle: fresh puzzle every 24 hours, leaderboard for fastest solve
- [ ] Puzzle creator: players can design and submit puzzles for community voting
- [ ] Hints system: spend 50 Gold to reveal one correct action
- [ ] Rewards: exclusive "Puzzle Master" card back, Stardust per puzzle tier completed
- [ ] Tutorial integration: first 10 puzzles teach advanced keyword interactions

### 8.2.3 2v2 Tag Team Mode
- [ ] Two-player teams share a combined health pool (50 HP) and board (7 slots each = 14 total)
- [ ] Players alternate turns within the team (P1-A → P1-B → P2-A → P2-B)
- [ ] Teammate's board is visible; limited "ping" communication during partner's turn
- [ ] Team synergy bonus: cards that reference "your ally" for cross-player effects
- [ ] Casual and Ranked queues for 2v2
- [ ] Exclusive "Tag Team" achievements and seasonal rewards

### 8.2.4 Boss Raids (Co-op PvE)
- [ ] 2-4 players cooperate against a massive boss with 200+ HP
- [ ] Boss has unique hero power, special rules, and phase transitions at HP thresholds
- [ ] Weekly boss rotation (4 bosses in the pool at launch)
- [ ] Raid rewards: exclusive cosmetics, bonus Stardust, unique card backs
- [ ] Boss mechanics require coordination (e.g., "Boss is immune unless a GUARDIAN is in play on both players' boards")
- [ ] Difficulty tiers: Normal, Heroic, Mythic (scaling HP, extra mechanics)

### 8.2.5 Chaos Mode
- [ ] Rotating random rule modifiers each game
- [ ] Example modifiers:
  - "All minions have random keywords"
  - "Spells cost (0) but deal half damage"
  - "All minions are 1/1 but have DOUBLE_STRIKE"
  - "Both players start with 10 crystals"
  - "Cards cost their Attack instead of their Crystal cost"
  - "Every minion has ECHO"
  - "Your deck is replaced with random cards from your opponent's faction"
- [ ] 2 modifiers active per match (randomly combined)
- [ ] No ranked impact — pure fun mode
- [ ] Casual rewards (reduced Gold, but exclusive Chaos card back for 50 wins)

### 8.2.6 Custom Rules Creator
- [ ] Players can create custom game rules from a menu of toggles and sliders
- [ ] Share custom rules as a 6-character code (importable)
- [ ] Popular custom formats featured on a community "Custom Games" browser
- [ ] Options: starting HP, starting crystals, max crystals, hand size, deck size, banned keywords, required keywords, banned cards, etc.
- [ ] "Pauper" format pre-built: Commons and Rares only

## 8.3 Collection & Cosmetic Depth

### 8.3.1 Card Upgrade Paths
- [ ] Standard → Golden (200/400/800/1600 Stardust by rarity)
- [ ] Golden → Prismatic (400/800/1600/3200 Stardust)
- [ ] Prismatic → Astral (800/1600/3200/6400 Stardust)
- [ ] Collection tracker: "X% of cards at Golden or above"
- [ ] Milestone rewards for upgrading X cards to each tier
- [ ] In-game visual indicator when opponent plays an Astral card (subtle awe reaction)

### 8.3.2 Board Pets / Mascots
- [ ] Small animated creatures that sit on your side of the board
- [ ] Idle animations: sleeping, exploring, reacting to plays
- [ ] React to game events: cheer on attacks, cower from damage, celebrate lethal
- [ ] 1 default pet per faction, additional pets unlockable via achievements/shop
- [ ] Pets have rarity tiers: Common (static), Rare (animated), Epic (interactive), Legendary (evolves during the match)
- [ ] Opponent can see your pet (social flex)

### 8.3.3 Victory Poses & Defeat Animations
- [ ] Winning hero strikes a faction-themed victory pose with voiceline
- [ ] 3 unlockable victory poses per hero (default + 2 earnable)
- [ ] Defeat animation: hero-specific (Pyroclast hero burns out, Voidborn fades into void)
- [ ] "GG" emote option during victory screen (sportsmanship reward: +10 Gold when used)

### 8.3.4 Extended Cosmetics
- [ ] **Card Sleeves** — Decorative card borders visible during gameplay (distinct from card backs)
- [ ] **Playmats** — Full board cosmetic overrides (beyond faction boards)
- [ ] **Emote Packs** — Themed emote bundles (Pirate emotes, Robot emotes, etc.)
- [ ] **Hero Skins** — Alternate hero portraits with new voicelines and hero power VFX
- [ ] **Finishers** — Custom lethal-blow animations (e.g., meteor strike, black hole, crystal shatter)
- [ ] All cosmetics previewable in a "Dressing Room" before purchase

- [ ] In-game codex with entries for each faction, planet, hero, and key characters
- [ ] Entries unlock through gameplay milestones (win 10 games as Pyroclast → unlock Ignaros lore)
- [ ] Animated planet dioramas for each faction's homeworld
- [ ] Character bios for all 10 heroes with backstory, motivations, and rivalries
- [ ] "Lore Hunter" achievement track with exclusive rewards

### 8.4.2 Card Voicelines
- [ ] All 20+ Legendary cards get play/attack/death voicelines (3 each)
- [ ] All 10 heroes get expanded voicelines (greetings, threats, wow, thanks, well played, oops)
- [ ] Interaction voicelines: specific cards react to each other when played (rivals, allies, lore connections)
- [ ] STARFORGE transformation has a unique voiceline ("I am reborn!", "Witness my true form!", etc.)
- [ ] Audio processing: even synthesized voices can sound alien/robotic/ethereal with faction-specific effects

### 8.4.3 Seasonal Story Events
- [ ] Quarterly narrative-driven limited events (2 weeks each)
- [ ] Example: "The Void Incursion" — Voidborn invade Luminar's planet, special PvE encounters
- [ ] Unique event cards (temporarily playable during the event, then added to collection permanently)
- [ ] Branching narrative choices that affect which rewards you earn
- [ ] Event-exclusive cosmetics (card back, board, pet) that never return
- [ ] Community-wide progress bar: all players contribute wins toward unlocking the event finale

### 8.4.4 Animated Card Lore
- [ ] Tap/hold a card's flavor text to trigger a 5-10 second animated lore vignette
- [ ] Comic-panel art style: 2-3 panels telling a micro-story about the card
- [ ] Legendaries get full animated scenes (15-20 seconds)
- [ ] Collected vignettes viewable in the Codex

## 8.5 Advanced Competitive Features

### 8.5.1 Ban/Pick Draft Mode
- [ ] Competitive format for tournaments and high-level ranked
- [ ] Each player bans 2 factions, then picks from the remaining 8
- [ ] Adds a strategic meta-gaming layer before the match even starts
- [ ] UI: split-screen draft phase with timers and faction portraits

### 8.5.2 Conquest Tournament Format
- [ ] Bring 3 decks (different factions), must win with each one to advance
- [ ] Losing deck is not eliminated — winning deck is removed from your lineup
- [ ] Standard competitive TCG tournament format (used in Hearthstone Masters, etc.)
- [ ] Integrated into the Tournament Mode as an alternate format option

### 8.5.3 Replay Analysis Tools
- [ ] Post-game "Win Probability Graph" showing how the game shifted turn-by-turn
- [ ] AI-highlighted "Pivotal Turns" — moments where the win probability swung 20%+
- [ ] "Suggested Play" — tap any turn in a replay to see what the AI would have done differently
- [ ] Heat map: which cards contributed most to the win/loss (damage dealt, value generated)
- [ ] Shareable replay links with embedded win probability graph

### 8.5.4 Faction Wars (Global Event)
- [ ] Seasonal event: all players pledge to one of the 10 factions
- [ ] Global win tracker: every ranked win contributes to your faction's score
- [ ] Real-time faction standings on a galactic map (planets glow based on faction dominance)
- [ ] Winning faction gets exclusive seasonal cosmetic (card back + board border)
- [ ] Top 100 contributors per faction get a unique title/badge
- [ ] Drives tribal identity and community engagement

### 8.5.5 Seasonal World Championship Arc
- [ ] 4-month competitive circuit integrated into the game
- [ ] Open qualifiers → Regional finals → World Championship
- [ ] In-game bracket viewer with live match results
- [ ] "Pick the Winner" predictions for community engagement (predict correct → earn Gold)
- [ ] Champion's card: winning player gets a card named after them in the next expansion

## 8.6 Social & Community

### 8.6.1 Mentor System
- [ ] Experienced players (Diamond+) can opt-in as mentors
- [ ] Mentors can spectate a mentee's game and draw arrows / highlight cards to suggest plays
- [ ] Mentees earn bonus XP, mentors earn exclusive "Teacher" cosmetics
- [ ] Mentor rating system: mentees rate their experience after each session

### 8.6.2 Deck Theorycraft Simulator
- [ ] Sandbox mode: play both sides of the board yourself
- [ ] Draw specific cards on demand (search and add to hand)
- [ ] Set up any board state manually (for testing interactions)
- [ ] "Goldfish" mode: play your deck against an empty opponent to test draw curves
- [ ] Shareable board state snapshots (useful for community discussion)

### 8.6.3 Community Card Voting
- [ ] Each expansion, the dev team designs 5 candidate cards
- [ ] Community votes on which 2 are included in the final set
- [ ] Voting period: 1 week, announced via in-game event
- [ ] Winning cards get a "Community Choice" watermark
- [ ] Drives player investment and ownership in the game's direction

### 8.6.4 Streamer & Content Creator Tools
- [ ] One-click "Streamer Mode" — hides account name, rank, and collection value
- [ ] Deck tracker overlay (built-in, toggleable): shows remaining cards in deck with probabilities
- [ ] "Highlight Reel" — auto-generates a 30-second clip of the best moments from a match
- [ ] Twitch extension: viewers can see your hand, vote on your next play, predict outcomes
- [ ] YouTube Shorts / TikTok export: auto-cropped vertical clips of lethal moments

## 8.7 Quality of Life

### 8.7.1 Post-Game Stats Screen
- [ ] Damage dealt (total, to hero, to minions)
- [ ] Cards drawn, cards played, cards destroyed
- [ ] Keywords triggered (how many times each keyword fired)
- [ ] MVP card (highest value generated)
- [ ] Crystals spent vs. crystals wasted (unspent crystals per turn)
- [ ] "Closest Moment" — the turn where the loser was closest to winning
- [ ] XP and reward breakdown

### 8.7.2 Smart Deck Suggestions
- [ ] "You're 3 cards away from a Tier-1 Pyroclast deck. Craft them for 800 Stardust?"
- [ ] Auto-fill: select a faction, and the system suggests a competitive deck from your collection
- [ ] "Budget" suggestions: best deck possible with your current cards (no crafting needed)
- [ ] Import meta decks from the community deck hub with one tap

### 8.7.3 Card History Timeline
- [ ] In-game sidebar showing a scrollable timeline of all cards played by both players
- [ ] Tap any entry to see the board state at that moment
- [ ] Chess-notation style: "T3: Pyroclast plays Flame Imp (3/2) → attacks Hero (3 dmg)"
- [ ] Exportable as text log for analysis

### 8.7.4 Undo Last Action (Casual Only)
- [ ] Single undo before ending turn (one per turn)
- [ ] Only in Casual and Friendly modes (never in Ranked/Tournament)
- [ ] Cannot undo after seeing hidden information (e.g., drawing a card)
- [ ] Reduces feel-bad moments for new players learning the game

### 8.7.5 Auto-Squelch & Social Preferences
- [ ] Global toggle: auto-mute all opponent emotes
- [ ] Per-game toggle: mute this specific opponent
- [ ] "Minimal UI" mode: hide all cosmetic effects (for competitive focus)
- [ ] Adjustable animation speed: Normal / Fast / Ultra-Fast (for experienced players)
